first identify your wifi card with the command 'ip a' wich will display something like the following. 

		$ ip a
		
		lo0: flags=8049 mtu 32768
			inet 127.0.0.1 netmask 0xff000000
			inet6 ::1 prefixlen 128
			inet6 fe80::1%lo0 prefixlen 64 scopeid 0x2
		
		em0: flags=8843 mtu 1500
			options=8
			inet 192.168.1.100 netmask 0xffffff00 broadcast 192.168.1.255
			inet6 fe80::212:4b00:7e5:e3c3%em0 prefixlen 64 scopeid 0x1
			ether 00:11:22:33:44:55

		iwx0: flags=8843 mtu 1500
			inet 192.168.1.100 netmask 0xffffff00 broadcast 192.168.1.255
			inet6 fe80::212:4b00:7e5:e3c3%iwx0 prefixlen 64 scopeid 0x1
			ether 00:11:22:33:44:55

In my case the WiFi adapter/card is the iwx0, read this article for more details on the supported chipsets available

Turn on your chipset 

		ifconfig iwx0 up

Using your favourite text editor, edit the file '/etc/hostname.iwx0' (in your case replace the iwx0 with the chipset you previously powered on) and paste the following: 

		join "networkID1" wpakey "networkPassword1"'
		join "networkID2" wpakey "networkPassword2"'
		dhcp

Note: you can put as many networks using 'join'.

Now restart your configurations with: 

		$ doas sh /etc/netstart iwx0
